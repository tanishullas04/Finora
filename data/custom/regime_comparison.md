# Regime Comparison – Old vs New (FY 2025–26)

This document provides a clear, simplified comparison between the Old Regime and the New Regime effective from 1 April 2025 (AY 2026–27).

---

## 1. Overview

### **New Tax Regime (Default Regime)**
- Basic exemption: **₹4,00,000**
- Slabs from **5% to 30%**
- **Rebate up to ₹12,00,000** taxable income
- Standard Deduction: **₹75,000**
- Very few deductions available

### **Old Tax Regime**
- Basic exemption: **₹2,50,000**
- Higher slab rates
- All major deductions allowed (80C, 80D, HRA, LTA, Home Loan, etc.)
- More paperwork but often lower tax for high‑deduction users

---

## 2. New vs Old Slab Comparison

### **New Regime Slabs (FY 2025–26)**
| Income Range | Tax Rate |
|--------------|----------|
| 0 – 4,00,000 | 0% |
| 4,00,001 – 8,00,000 | 5% |
| 8,00,001 – 12,00,000 | 10% |
| 12,00,001 – 16,00,000 | 15% |
| 16,00,001 – 20,00,000 | 20% |
| 20,00,001 – 24,00,000 | 25% |
| 24,00,000+ | 30% |

### **Old Regime Slabs**
(unchanged from earlier years)

---

## 3. Which Regime Should You Choose?

### **Choose New Regime If:**
- You don’t claim many deductions  
- Your taxable income is **below ₹12 lakh**
- You are a new employee without investments
- You want simple tax filing

### **Choose Old Regime If:**
- Your deductions exceed **₹2 lakh** (80C + 80D + HRA + home loan)
- You have a home loan (big interest deduction)
- You pay high rent (HRA benefit)
- You invest heavily in Section 80C, NPS, insurance
